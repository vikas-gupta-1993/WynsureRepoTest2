Glossary

 

This glossary reuses the Wagram Glossary, which defines generic words like "Operation", "Rule Engine", "Business Process"...

In any documentation Wynsure uses upper case letter to start each of the glossary words, so that the reader understands that a specific definition exists.

 

·                    Account: list of Account Lines and associated balance. An Actor may have 0 or several Accounts.

·                    Account Line: financial Debit or Credit on an Account.

·                    Account Representative: see Client Representative.

·                    Actor: Person or Legal Entity.

·                    Administrative Service: Service which is not a Coverage (like several copies of the same Bill to different addresses).

·                    Agent: Distributor who sells products from one Issuing Company.

·                    Amendment: Contract Modification, which requires a signature.

·                    Assuming Company: see Reinsurer.

·                    Assuming Treaties: Legal Agreement for a Reinsurer where the Reinsurer will receive Risks from a Ceding Company.

·                    Automatic Acceptance Limit: see Automatic Binding Limit.

·                    Automatic Binding Limit: The maximum amount the Reinsurer will accept without making its own Underwriting assessment. Also called Automatic Acceptance Limit.

·                    Beneficiary: Person or Legal entity designated by the Policy Owner to be the recipient(s) of a Benefit. For example, the employee designates the spouse as primary Beneficiary for 100% of the proceeds for Basic Life Benefit.

·                    Benefit: defines what is Delivered to the Beneficiary if the Risk happens.

·                    Benefit Evaluation Rule: Business Rule that defines the Delivered Benefit.

·                    Bill: defines the list of Service charges that the Payer must pay to the Insurer to validate the Contract; a way to tell the Payer the amount they owe for Services.

·                    Broker or Brokerage Firm: a State-licensed Person or Legal Entity who places business with several Insurers and who represents the Insurance Subscriber rather than the Insurer, even though the Broker is paid commissions by the Insurer.

·                    Business Transaction: In Reinsurance, it is a cession change that can impact the Reinsured Amount. For example, it can be a “new Business Transaction” when it is a new subscription, or “termination Business transaction” when the Risk is not reinsured any more.

·                    Carrier: see Insurer.

·                    Ceding Company: the company that transfers its Risk to a Reinsurer.  Also called the Cedant.

·                    Ceding Treaty: Reinsurance Agreement between the Direct Insurer and the Reinsurer.

·                    Census Information: Employee/Member and dependent data such as name, date of birth, gender, used in rating, underwriting, and enrolling Persons in a Group.

·                    Certificate of Insurance: a document delivered to the insured person, as part of the group policy that summarizes the benefits and principal provisions of the policy.

·                    Cession: Records of all the reinsured coverage information such as starting reinsurance date, Reinsurance Amount, Face Amount, insured date of birth, insured name... The Ceding Company sends this record to the Reinsurer.

·                    Chosen Member Service: Individual Services chosen by the Group and included in the Member Product. There is one Chosen Member Services for each Offered Member service. The Chosen Member Service may be used to include business rules, which do not apply to members like "termination of the period when it is allowed to subscribe this service" or "price to the group the right to propose this Service".

·                    Claim: request to get Delivered Benefit(s) because a Risk occurred.

·                    Claimant: see Beneficiary.

·                    Classification: categorize employees/members of an Organization for purposes such as eligibility, rating, benefits, billing. Ex: Size of a company.

·                    Classification Value: value of a Classification. Ex: small, average, large.

·                    Client: Person or Legal Entity to which the Product is sold. Can be different from Subscriber, Payer, Beneficiary.

·                    Client Representative: The Insurer employee compensated for assisting the Sales Representative and providing on-going customer service from the field office. Also known as Account Representative.

·                    Company: see Legal Entity.

·                    Compensation Plan: see Distribution Product.

·                    Contingent Beneficiary: The recipient of the Benefit proceeds if the primary Beneficiary has died.

·                    Contract: legal binding agreement between a Subscriber and an Issuing Company (see Policy).

·                    Contract Modification: modification of a Contract, which can be an Endorsement or an Amendment.

·                    Coverage: Insurance Service.

·                    Coverage Amount: the amount payable by an Insurer to a claimant, assignee, or beneficiary under each Coverage.

·                    Coverage Status: current state of an individual Subscribed Service/Coverage, i.e. active, terminated, pending, lapsed, ineligible.

·                    Coverage Type: In a Treaty it is generic reinsurance agreements terms on a particularly Coverage. It contains the default rules for Retention, Premium calculation and duration.

·                    Covered Person: a Person who is insured under a Subscribed Coverage. For example, on John Doe’s dental coverage the Covered Persons are himself, his spouse, and his child.

·                    Delivery Check Rule: Business Rule, which checks if Benefit Delivery is allowed.

·                    Delivered Benefit: defines the Benefit Delivered by the Provider to the Beneficiary when a risk has happened.

·                    Delivered Service: set of Delivered Benefits which are associated to a Subscribed Service

·                    Dependent: Person related to another Person, who depends on that Person’s financial support.

·                    Dependent Coverage: A Coverage that is based on another eligible Person being Covered (employee/member). If the employee/member Coverage terminates, the Dependent Coverage will also terminate.

·                    Distributor: Person or Legal Entity, which sells Policies (Agent, Broker, Partnering Bank...) coming from an Issuing Company.

·                    Distribution Contract: Contract between a Distributor and the Insurer (defines roles, duties and commissions).

·                    Distribution Product: Product proposed to a Distributor.

·                    Direct Insurer: Insurer that sells insurance Products to Group Member or Individual Person.

·                    Dividend: disbursement of surplus premium, a type of Benefit Option.

·                    Effective Date: date on which the Service/Coverage becomes in force.

·                    Eligibility Rule: evaluate when a Person is qualified to enroll in (or Subscribe) a Coverage.

·                    Endorsement: Contract Modification, which does not require a signature.

·                    Enrollment: Subscription of a Member to Services of a Group Benefit Plan. Offered services are usually part of the employee’s Group Benefit Plan but could also be offered on an individual basis.

·                    Excess of Retention: Type of proportional reinsurance in which a Ceding Company establishes an amount Retention Limit, and a Reinsurer agrees to assume amounts over this limit, up to the Reinsurer's Automatic Binding Limit.

·                    Face Amount: Benefit amount paid by the Insurer in case of death or at the maturity of the Policy.

·                    Fee: amount of money due by a Payer which is not already included in the Service Price, i.e. $1.00 per individual bill, $300.00 per report, etc.

·                    General Ledger: corporate financial accounting system.

·                    Grace Period: The number of days following the due date that payment of Premium can be received before Coverage lapses.

·                    Group: Legal Entity that subscribes a Group Benefit Plan for his members; the members must have an affinity that allows them to be treated as a Group by a Carrier who offers Group insurance and who are legally entitled to the tax-preferred treatment of Group insurance.

·                    Group Benefit Plan (GBP): Policy subscribed by the Group to deliver Services to Members of the Group. Includes Subscribed Legal Entity Services and Chosen Member Services.

·                    Group Product: Product offered to a Legal Entity

·                    Group Service: Legal Entity Service included in a Group Benefit Plan (ex: statistics provided by the Insurer to the Group every month); different from Offered Member service.

·                    Guaranteed date: the date the Guarantee Period starts.

·                    Guarantee Period: The length of time the rates are guaranteed, starting from the Guaranteed Date.

·                    Guaranteed Rate: maximum Rate defined on an Offered Coverage used for reporting purposes – not rating.

·                    Holder: see Subscriber.

·                    Human resource Administrator: the Person within the Group who is responsible for the administration of a group benefit plan.

·                    Individual: see Person.

·                    Individual Bill: A bill delivered to an individual or household that displays premium due for each coverage and a total premium due.

·                    Individual Contract:

o                                           the Insurance Contract between an Issuing Company and a Person,

o                                           or a document prepared and issued to an insured person which represents the legal agreement.

·                    Individual Product: Product proposed to a Person.

·                    Individual Service: Service proposed to a Person.

·                    Insurance Professional: Employee who has Insurance Knowledge.

·                    Insured: person who is Covered for Insurance under the Insurance Contract and to whom, or on behalf of, the Insurer agrees to pay Benefits. See also Covered Person.

·                    Insurer: Legal Entity whose business is financing Insurance Benefits.

·                    Issuing Company: Legal entity which provides Insurance Contracts.

·                    Job: description of a Person’s responsibility to perform duties within an organization.

·                    Late Payment Process: process by which the system tracks unpaid Bills and issues notices to the Payer of payments not received. Based on an assigned timeline, Wynsure will eventually terminate Coverage that goes unpaid. Lapsing can be done only for a coverage, not for a service; lapse period has to be defined on each Coverage; need to be able to hold or cancel lapsing process.

·                    Legal Agreement: see Treaty.

·                    Legal Entity: Organization which has legal ability to sign a Contract (can be a Company or an Association or a Governmental entity); a Legal Entity may play different roles like Issuing company, Insurer, Provider, Distributor or Group Client.

·                    Legal Entity Product: Product proposed to a Legal Entity.

·                    Line of Business (LOB): a way of categorizing Offered Coverage to capture Premium, Fees, and Claims.

·                    List Bill: specific type of Billing Statement that displays detail at the Person and Coverage level.

·                    Lock Box: location within a designated bank where all Payers’ premium and fees are received and processed.

·                     Marketing Date: begin and end dates when a product can be sold.

·                    Member: a Person who is affiliated with or employed by a Legal Entity and is eligible to be enrolled in a Group Benefit Plan.

·                    Member Contract: Contract subscribed by a Member related to a Group Benefit Plan.

·                    Member Product: defines Offered Individual Services for Members; each GBP automatically generates a Member Product.

·                    Member Record: see Member Contract.

·                     Member Service: Individual Service proposed to Member in a Group Benefit Plan.

·                    Miscellaneous Fee: Fee of which amount is defined on line by the user for general purpose.

·                    Movement: Financial Operation (may be a bill, a payment…)

·                    Offered Service: defines how to Subscribe a Service; an Offered Service is linked to a Product.

·                    Offered Employee Service: Offered Service for which only Members are eligible.

·                    Offered Group Service: Offered Service for which only Organizations are eligible.

·                    On-Going Enrollment: Enrollment that occurs for new hires, late entrants, family status changes, or for increases to coverage.

·                    Open Enrollment: A special enrollment period offered to Employees/Members, perhaps to increase participation.

·                    Option: non-mandatory Service. It can be Optional (not proposed by default) or Proposed (proposed by default).

·                    Organization: structure of a Legal Entity, grouping together and breaking out smaller units of the whole for Billing and reporting purposes; also used to set up rules of the Group Benefit Plan. This could be subsidiary companies of a parent company, plant locations, employment category, payroll types etc. For example, if a Group Benefit Plan will offer different rates for smoker versus non-smoker, each of those categories would be set up as an Organization.

·                    Owner: person responsible for exercising full rights of the Policy. He is usually the Subscriber.

·                    Participant: A Reinsurer of a Pool. A Participant receives a specified percentage of each Risk ceded to the Pool.

·                    Payer: Person or Legal Entity who receives Bills and make Payments.

·                    Payment: Money received from the Payer and deposited into an Insurer bank account.

·                    Payment Form: The manner in which Payment is submitted and received, i.e. cash, check, ACH, wire transfer.

·                    Person: human being; a Person may play different roles (Subscriber, Beneficiary, Payer, Member of one or several Group Legal Entities).

·                    Policy: Contract between an Issuing Company and an Insurance Subscriber; the Policy includes Subscribed Services.

·                    Policy Status: a Policy may have several Status values:

o                                           Active,

o                                           Project (example: for a quote),

o                                           Terminated,

o                                           Never in Force: Project which never became Active,

o                                           On hold: an Active Policy suspended for first agreement,

o                                           On hold after Renewal: an Active Policy suspended for following agreement.

·                    Pool: is a group of Reinsurers. The Reinsurers belonging to a Pool are called Participants.

·                    Premium: An amount computed by the rating engine for a Coverage.

·                    Prepared Member Product: set of Member Services proposed for a GBP.

·                    Prepared Member Service: Member Service proposed for a GBP.

·                    Producer: see Distributor.

·                    Product: set of Services offered by an Insurer like Individual Product, Legal entity Product, or Distribution Product; Individual Product and Legal Entity Product must be filed to get State approvals.

·                    Product Factory: allows Insurance Professional to directly create and/or modify Insurance Products. These new Products are automatically available without any additional application development.

Delivered Benefit

·                    Provided Service: see Delivered Service.

·                    Provider: a Legal Entity with whom the Insurer has contracted to administer and/or deliver Service (can be different from Insurer). It is sometimes called Administrator.

·                    Provision: A part (clause, sentence, paragraph, etc.) of an insurance contract that describes or explains a feature, Benefit, condition, requirement, etc. of the insurance protection afforded by the Contract.

·                    Quota Share: Type of proportional Reinsurance in which the Reinsurer agrees to accept a certain percentage of Risk, and a Ceding Company retains the remaining percentage, up to a maximum Retention Limit.

·                    Recipient: Payer.

·                    Reconciliation: process by which we compare and settle that Payment amounts received for Bills are correct.

·                    Reinstatement: following a termination, a Contract, or Service can be reactivated after Underwriting approval, if the Reinstatement Date is before the delay after termination.

·                    Reinsurance: The transfer of some or all of an insurance Risk to another insurer.  The company transferring the risk is called the Ceding Company; the company receiving the risk is called the Reinsurer or Assuming Company.

·                    Reinsurance Agreement: see Treaty.

·                    Reinsurance Coverage: Related to a Coverage Type, it describes more precisely the Coverage reinsured. It defines for example the ceding company coverage Id and it can customize the default rules set up in the Coverage type (Premium calculation, Retention Rule…).

·                    Reinsurance Pool: see Pool.

·                    Reinsurance Risk: the chance of loss. It also used to refer the insured or the property covered by a Policy.

·                    Reinsured Amount: Amount reinsured by the Reinsurer.

·                    Reinsurer: An insurance company that accepts the Risk transferred from another insurance company in a reinsurance transaction.  Also called the Assuming Company.  

·                    Self Administration:

Method of reinsurance administration in which the Ceding Company maintains detailed records for each ceded policy and provides the Reinsurer with periodic reports outlining the Risk ceded and Premiums due.

·                    Remittance Form: a document completed by the Payer and remitted along with a payment. Could be, or include, a modified copy of the Bill.

·                    Renewal notice: amount of time prior to the renewal date at which a notice will need to be sent to the Contract’s Subscriber.

·                    Retention Rules: In Reinsurance, rules to defined the amount retained by the Ceding Company. See Quota Share, Excess Retention.

·                    Retention Limit: The maximum amount that an insurer is willing to carry at its own Risk without transferring some of the risk to a Reinsurer.

·                    Retro Treaty: Legal Agreement for a Reinsurer, where the Reinsurer plays the role of a Ceding Company and cedes a portion of its Risks.

·                    Reverse: Backing out an existing Bill, to generate a back Bill and reverse entries in general ledger (see also back-out bill).

·                    Rider: Benefit Amount modification saved in a specific record.

·                    Risk: event that triggers Delivered Benefits.

·                    Sales representative: the Insurer Employee compensated for the distribution of Services and Coverages.

·                    Seller: Organization that proposes Products to Groups.

·                    Service: what is Subscribed (like Coverage or Administrative Service); see Offered Service, Subscribed Service and Delivered service. A Subscribed Service gives right to one or several Deliverable Benefits

·                    Sponsor: Person who signs the Group Benefit Plan on behalf of the Group.

·                    State Approval: action of consent by the appropriate state insurance department(s) allowing sale of specific insurance Products and issue of related certificates/Policies. All insurance Policies and all provisions contained within those Policies require approval by the appropriate state insurance department(s) before they can be sold or issued.

·                    Status: see Policy Status.

·                    Subscribed Service: Service which is Subscribed by a Subscriber inside a Contract.

·                    Subscriber: Person or Legal Entity which signs the Contract.

·                    Subscription Check Rule: Business Rule which checks if Subscription (or other actions like modification, suspension...) of a Contract is allowed.

§         The rule may be defined globally at Contract level or at Subscribed Service level.

§         Distinction between Eligibility Rules (check that Subscriber or Beneficiaries are allowed) and Dependency rules (check dependencies between Services).

·                    Supplemental Coverage: Coverage type that indicates the Coverage is in addition to basic Coverage. Subscription to Basic Coverage would be a pre-requirement to subscribing to Supplemental Coverage. The Employee pays 100% of the Premium. This coverage is always optional for the employee.

·                    Suspended Payment: Payment received that has not been applied to a specific Account – Unidentified Payments and New Case.

·                     Termination: a Contract terminates when it is not renewed by the Subscriber or the Insurer. A Terminated Contract can be Reinstated (see Policy Status).

·                    Third Party Administrator: Legal Entity that Administers or Delivers one or more Services for a Group, within a Group Benefit Plan.

·                    Treaty: a transaction between two insurance companies one company, called the Ceding Company, transfers some of its insurance Risk to the other company, called the Reinsurer. It is also called “reinsurance agreement” or “legal agreement”.

·                    Underwriter: Insurer Employee responsible for assessing the risk a proposed group/insured represents and applying rating rules based on the level of risk.

·                    Voluntary Coverage: Coverage type that indicates the Coverage is optional for an employee/member/covered person and Subscription to Basic Coverage is not a pre-requirement. The employee/member/covered person pays 100% of the premium.

·                    Waiver of Premium: provision that, under certain conditions, a Person’s insurance will be kept in full force by the Insurer without Payment of Premiums.
