Wynsure Product/Contract factory

The Insurance Product Factory allows the Insurance Business peopleProfessionals to directly create and modify Insurance products. These new products are automatically available for policy management (subscription, etc.), billing, and claim management without any additional application development.

 

The Insurance Product factory makes use of the framework component "Product Factory".


Before defining the processes to define Insurance Products, let's define the Objects of the Product Factory.

 

            The concept of service is the driving concept of the Product Factory, for the service is what is defining the business of the company. In Wynsure, the service consists mostly of coverages, since coverages is what the company is selling to its customer.

                                                                   

The insurance model describes 2 dimensions:

3 columns :

Offered (What is defined by marketing managers)

Subscribed (what is subscribed or modified by the customerSubscriber)

Delivered (what is delivered by the company Provider when a Cclaim occurs).

There is a chronological order between these concepts: What is offered is then what is subscribed, and what is subscribed is then what can be delivered.

 

3 lines :

Product that is a packaging of different services,

Service that is what is actually driving the business of the company.

Group of Services that is a grouping of services for sharing common behavior. The use of group of services is optional.

 

The same model applies to many insurance functions and domains:

Iindividual Insurance

Legal Entity Insurance

Group Insurance

Reinsurance

Compensation

 

Services include

cCoverage (payment of benefits when a covered loss occurs)

Benefit Options (like Waiver of Premiums)

Aadministrative Sservices (like: send the same bill to 3 addresses).


Pproducts and Ooffered Sservices are defined through different sets of business rules to allow business people to parameterize different behaviors

-          Pricing rules for calculating premiums during the billing process

-          Subscription rules for calculating the eligibility during the subscription/enrollment process.

-          Coverage amount rules for calculating the coverage amount during the subscription/enrollment process.

-          Underwriting rules for balancing between automatic underwriting and manual underwriting during the subscription/enrollment process.

-          Benefit rules for calculating the benefit to be paid to the claimant during the claim process.

Product

Definition

A product defines is the definition ofwhat is subscribed in a contract and what is delivered afterwards. The product defines the data and processes that are shared between by every contracts subscribing related to this product.
An insurance product can be described as a structure of offered services. Each offered service defines the behaviour of the corresponding subscribed service part.

Defined behaviours are:

1.                                          Subscription check: Eligibility rules, that checkss if the Ccontract can be subscribed (includes Eligibility Rules or Dependency rules)

2.                                          Delivery Check: checks if and  Sservices can be Ddelivered

3.                                          Pricing rule, that calculates the price of the Contract/Services: Subscription Pricing Rule or Periodic Pricing Rule or Modification Pricing Rule

4.                                          Service Evaluation or Benefit that calculates the benefit when a service is delivered (it can be the amount of reimbursement)

5.                                          Contract Number Generation

6.                                          Some Billing Behavior



 

If necessary, all these rules may also be used at the Product or Group of Services level if necessary through a customization of these components.

A product is attached to an issuing company. The issuing company needs to be manually chosen in case Wynsure is managing more than one issuing company.

Usage

The contract is the subscription of a product, hence for every type of contract that needs to be managedy, a type of product define needs to be managed as well.

·         Insurance individual products

·         Insurance Group products

·         Group Benefit Plan Products

·         Compensation Plans to manage Compensation contracts that defines the relation between the company forgiving the customerDistributor and the Insurercompany that signed the contract.

·         Reinsurance products to manage reinsurance contract between the Issuing company and reinsurers.

 

Individual Product includes Offered Individual services.

Legal Entity Product includes Offered Legal entity services.

Group Benefit Plans Product consists of 2 products

-          GBP Member Model Product includes Offered Individual services specifically created to be part of a GBP contract. These services will be used for member enrollment

-          GBP Product includes Offered Legal entity services like a Legal Entity product

 

In a GBP, the Group may subscribe Legal Entity Services for the group itself (like sent statistics every end of month), but the most important part is to create a Member Product which will allow the Members of the Group to subscribe (or Enrol) to this Member Product. The services proposed to the Member may be chosen from all Individual Services already defined in Individual Products and/or Prepared Member Products specifically designed for this usage. For each Offered Member Service inside the Member Product, there is a Chosen Member service inside the GBP which allows it to store Group rules, but not Member rules.

 

 

Main screens of Insurance Product

 

Definition Folder

This folder allows the creation of the services defining the product.

One can create new services, or consult/modify/remove existing services. A selected service can also be copied to create a new service from an existing one.

It is possible to directly access the different rules of each service by opening the tree node of a service.

 

Administration folder

The administration tab present various general information about the product:

-          Effective date enforces from when the product can be subscribed. If the effective date is left blank, the product can not be subscribed

-          End date enforces when the product is no longer available for sale

-          Reinstatement delay: When a contract is terminated, it is possible to reinstate it if the reinstatement date is before the delay after termination defined in the product.

-          Currency is the currency of all contracts for that product. Also all amounts manipulated by rules are using this currency. Bills, Payments, Compensation, etc. are managed in the currency defined in the product.

-           

-          Period concerned by billing: Billing can be processed based on previous period, so when a period is terminatedended, the billing module calculates the bill on this period (Periods are billed when terminated).
Billing can also be processed based on the next period, so before a period starts, the billing module calculates the associated bill (Periods are billed in advance).

-          Contract # Generator: each product is responsible of assigning contract numbers using a generator. If no generator is specified, the global Contract # generator defined in the issuing company will be used.

 

Description folder

The description folder allows to enter a free text description of the product. It can be the commercial summary of the product, for instance.

 

State approvals folder

The state approval folder allows to manage the filing of the product in the states. It is possible for a product to say that it is :

-          authorized in all states

-          authorized in no states

-          authorized in detail states. Individual approvals must be entered. Theses approvals are entered for one or many states and for a period of time.

 

Product state approval screen

Several state approvals can be defined. One state approval is based on one list of states that are approved for on a period between Effective date and End date.

When end date is not defined, it means that the period end is unknown.

 

State can be checked for both individual and Group subscriptions. In case of Group insurance, you can specify if the state approval the Situs state. In case of individual insurance you can choose Residence State.

 

State approval application:

For group products, State approval rules are used in Group Benefit Plan definition module to filter products that are not authorized for the Group (depending on situs state and worksite state of the group). Members enrolments dont check the state approval.
For individual: State approval rules are checked during activation of the contract. If the product is not approved for the specified state of the subscriber, the activation is refused.

Subscription rule

 

Eligibility folder

The subscription rule consists of

-          subscription behavior

-          eligibility rules

-          dependencies rules

 

The subscription behaviour of an offered service can be:

· Mandatory
When the product is subscribed, (when a contract is created), the service is automatically subscribed and cannot be removed.

· Optional
When the product is subscribed, (when a contract is created), the optional service is not automatically subscribed, but the user can manually add this optionavailable for subscription.

· Proposed
When the product is subscribed, (when a contract is created), the optional service is automatically subscribed, but the user can manually remove this option.

 

Dependancies folder

·Mandatory
When the product is subscribed, so when a contract is created, the service is automatically subscribed and cannot be removed.

·
Dependency with other options:

When the product designer creates a new product option, he has to specify the interaction of this new option with existing options of the same product.

The new option can be incompatible with some existing options: the option cannot be subscribed if one of the excluded options is already subscribed.

The new option can be subscribed only if one or several other options are already subscribed: the option requires a list of other options of the same product.

Calculation folder

The different possibilities of calculation of one subscriber Coverage Amount may be computed by several methods: are :

·                         Flat amount, predefined amount (predefined in the offered coverage rule)

·                         Earnings based, on the subscribers earnings (Used in Group insurance))

The earnings of the subscriber can be defined as

§                        Base salary

§                        Base salary + bonus

§                        Base salary + commission

§                        Base salary + bonus + commission

·                         Table based

·                         It is based on the coverage Amount of another subscribed service

In this case, it is possible to adjust the Coverage amount with a simple flat coefficient orf with a coefficient coming from a table so depending on the subscriber characteristics (For instance smokers will have a smaller coverage amount than non smokers)

Min/Max folder

The product designer can specify some limits for coverage amount

Several strategies can be used and combined to establish a minimum or maximum value for the resulting coverage amount

The mMinimum is calculated as:

·         A flat amount

·         A % or multiple of subscriber earnings

The maximum is calculated as:

·         A fFlat amount

·         A % of the coverage amount of an other subscribed service

·         A % or multiple of subscriber earnings


When the maximum rule is applied, the used coverage amount to be checked can be combined with the coverage amount of another already subscribed service. In this case, the system checks that the sum of current service coverage amount and the other service coverage amount passes the maximum rule.

 

When the Coverage amount calculation rule is based on the subscriber earnings, it is possible to limit the earnings amount taken into account.

Reduction folder

If defined, a reduction rule will be applied automatically by the Contract Maintenance Batch every days. It affects the global coverage amount of a subscribed coverage. After subscription, the coverage amount will be decreased depending on a rule that can be based on available business data (like subscriber age for instance).
The product designer can specify for one coverage two reduction rules. He can specify which of these two rules will be applied: the lesser result of the two rules or the greater one.


The reduction rule table can be defined as:

·         A % of the coverage amount

·         A % of the earnings of the subscriber

·         A flat reduction amount

Underwriting rule

When a coverage with coverage amount subscribed, the coverage amount needs to be approved. It can be approved automatically by Wynsure or must be approved manually by an authorized user.
The underwriting rule defines the part of the coverage amount that is automatically approved.

Content folder

One option is: the coverage amount has to be fully underwritten if the member enrolment is after a specific delayhappens after the eligibility date.
Then the maximum can be specified as a combination of:

·                         The coverage amount over a specified flat limit must be underwritten

·                         The coverage amount over a % of the salary must be underwritten

 

It is also possible to define the way a coverage amount increases (when the coverage is already subscribed) needs to be underwritten:

·                         Any increase needs to be underwritten

·                         Only increase of a % of the salary

Coverages for group product have specific screens for underwriting rules

 

Underwriting rules for Group coverages

In case of Group Insurance, the product designer can defined several Underwriting Rules depending on the enrolment period.

 

Four enrolment periods are defined:

·         Initial enrolment: for later use

·         Annual enrolment: it is a period that will be available every year for new member enrolment.

·         Open enrolment: it is a period specifically defined by the product designer (Begin/End date for this period must be entered)

·         Ongoing enrolment: all other enrolment that are not into the annual period, the open period or the initial period.

 

 

So annual enrollments and open enrollments depends on periods that must be defined globally for the underwriting rules

-          Annual enrollment period: it is an annual period therefore only day and month need to be specified

-          Open enrollment period: it is a unique period so day, month and year need to be specified.

Example of Offered services

LTD

Long term disability coverage provides wage replacement to individuals who experience wage loss due to a disability. LTD coverage lasts for at least one year.

STD

Replaces a portion of lost earnings for a limited time, typically less than one year.

Health, dental, vision

Pays some or all costs of medical treatment.

Waiver of premium

It is an insurance policy clause or optionService allowing a policyholder who becomes disabled or seriously ill not to not pay the premiums.

Conversion

All group policies allow an employee to convert to an individual policy.
Benefits and premiums can change (prescription medications are not covered under most converted policies) and the insured is responsible for paying all premiums.

Portability

Insurance Portability protects insurance coverage for workers and their families when they change or lose their jobs.

Future purchase option

With this option, the subscriber gets an inflation increase every predefined period. It is a kind of additional long term care insurance coverage whose premium can be based on the age of the subscriber at the time the increase takes effect.

Accelerated life

This option allows the subscriber to be paid an amount when he is diagnosed with a Terminal Condition and have a short life expectancy.

Continuation

This option allows employees to continue their group insurance coverage under certain conditions after their employment has terminated.

Formula

 

 

 

The rule editor is displaying a screen consisting of different areas:

-          on the top, and area to give the name and the description of the rule.

-          on the right, a text area that contains the rule itself

-          on the left, a tree box that contains all available resources to build the rule.

 

The purpose of the rule editor is that it is very easy to write a rule.

All the text can be built doing a dragn drop from the resources on the right.

 

The resources fall in different categories

-          Business Data: dynamic information that can be used in the rule

-          Business Indexes: indexes are maintained separately and behaves like a history of constant value.

-          Operators: syntax elements to manipulate values

-          Statements: algorithm keywords like for .. endFor, ifelseendIf, etc

-          Functions: library of mathematical and scientific functions.

-          Tables: custom built library of tables that can be reused in a formula

-          Rules: custom built library of rules that can be reused in a formula

The status of the rule is displayed beside this button. It can be:

-          needs parsing

-          syntax errors

-          rule OK.
