Modifying a Product

It is very easy to create a product: You create some services (coverage for insurance) then you have to define the rules attached to this coverage:

Rules can be Eligibility, rating, Benefit calculation,

In the generic Product factory the rules can be attached at the product level, at the service level or at the Benefit level.

In Wynsure (the insurance solution) the rules are only defined at coverage level and benefit level.

Rating and eligibility at Coverage level and Benefit Calculation at Benefit level.

 

Take the example of life Coverage

Several rules are defined:

o        The coverage amount rule calculates the coverage amount of the employee (can be chosen or for instance calculated from his salary)

o        The Underwriting rule determines if the validation of the employee contract requires a manual validation of an authorized user (an underwriter).

o        The rating rule defines the calculation of the price one member will have to pay if the coverage is subscribed.

o        The eligibility rule define who can subscribe the coverage (generally based on age in the insurance domain)

o        For each benefit, we define the benefit calculation rule that calculates the amount given to a claimant in case of loss.

 

Let us zoom on the rating rule

In our example the price is based on a table:

Depending on the age of the subscriber and the subscribed coverage amount, the price is different and defined into each cells of the table.

 

Every time you need a table, it is very simple :

o        First you choose the dimension of your table (here age and coverage amount). You can choose as many dimension as you want (If more than 2 dimensions; the edition is done by defining n-2 dimensions and working in a plan for the two last dimensions)

o        You have then to define the key values of the table dimensions: in our case 20 and 50 years old for the age and 100000$, 500000$ and 1000000$ (With the indexes button).

o        Then you can define the price for every case in the dynamic table editor.

 

If the rating is not based on a table, we have others possibilities :

Flat rate

You have just to define the price (simplest rule)

Formula rate

In this case you can define an algorithm in the rule editor:

Define the name of the rule: AgeBasedRating

From statement drag and drop Result

From Business data drag and drop BenefitAmount

Show available statements and Functions

 

The syntax Check button verify that your algorithm is OK , if an error appends, you see the error message and the line where there is the error :

select the error and see in the editor where is the error

 

Correct the error and parse again

When the rule is OK you can validate

 

Consult the Coverage to see the textual synthesis

Wynsure is able to generate a readable description of every rule. Then it is very easy to generate a detailed documentation of every products defined in wynsure.

You are now in front of the Pricing rule screen.

We can see that:

         The price is defined for one month of coverage

         Only characteristics of the subscriber are taken into account (It can depend on attached covered persons too)

         The price at subscription is prorated on the effective date of the subscribed coverage(Price is based on the exact number of days of coverage)

         The price of last coverage period, when the contract is terminated, is prorated on the effective date of the coverage termination too.

         The price is defined based on a Table combining the age of the subscriber and the coverage amount: for instance for a coverage amount of 200000$ and a subscriber of 30 years old, the price of one month of coverage is 450$

 

This pricing rule is applied to every kind of subscriber: all classifications

This rule will apply to all states.

 

The group will have to pay 40% of the due premium and the member contribution will be 60%. No limit is defined for the Group contribution.

Reconciliation Process

The reconciliation process is done from the Client account manager. You can access it by selecting the Client accounts folder in the ANC summary screen.

From the client accounts list, select the invoice account (It is the account attached to the GBP to manage bills and payments. This account was automatically created when the GBP was created)

Then click on Consult button

From this Account viewer you can access on bills attached to the account, lines, payments, and Operations that have been processed on the concerned account.

We can see that the balance of the account is 1400$ that means that ANC has a debit of 1400$.

-1000$ are suspended to the account, that means that 1000$ are not used to pay some due.

 

Click on Reconcile button to start the reconciliation process.

From the reconciliation screen, you can select the origin of the money you want to apply to due lines. To do it you must check the Source manually specified field. If not the money will be automatically taken from available payments. In our case, we do not change anything, as only one payment exists.

You must select the due line you want to reconcile.

The amount that will be attached to this due line is automatically updated with the maximum of what can be reconciled (regarding the available payment amount)

Then you can click on Reconcile Button.

As the due line is fully reconciled, it disappear from the list of available due line that can be reconciled.

 

You can now validate this process by clicking on OK button. (If you chose cancel button, the reconciliation will not be applied!)

The suspended amount is now 400$ as 600$ of suspended amount has been attached to the bill.

Set the Issuing company criteria to checked, and select Issuing company 1

Then click on search button. You can see two accounts between ANC and Issuing company 1. select the Suspense account and click on select button.

We are going to move all suspended amount to the suspense account. So click on <= button to do it.

Then click on Move button

The payment line disappears from the From line list as no more amount is available.

You can now validate your move money by clicking on the Apply button. If you choose Suspend button, the operation will be suspended into your To do list. You will be able later to find it back and continue the move money process, or forward it to someone else that is authorized to do it for instance (If you are not authorized to move this amount).
