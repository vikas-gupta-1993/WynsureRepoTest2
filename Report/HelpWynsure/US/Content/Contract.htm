Individual Life Contract main functions

The Individual Life insurance Contract is an agreement between a Person and an Issuing Company to get Benefits against life events (Death, Long Term Disability, Dental, etc).

In Wynsure, the Contract is created after selecting a Product and the Subscriber may choose among Coverages Offered by this Product.

Individual Contract and Subscribed Service Life Cycle

 

An Individual Contract and a Subscribed Service (Coverages as well as Administrative Services) have a specific life cycle identified by a Contract status :

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


The different Policy Status are:

·         Project: Temporary status that does not bind the Subscriber with the Issuing Company. Can be used as a quote. Like a draft, information does not need to be consistent. It is not taken into account by the Billing and no Service (for instance, Benefits) can be Delivered.

·         Active: The agreement between the Subscriber and the Issuing Company has been reached. Services can be Billed and Delivered.

·         Terminated: The Contract/Subscribed Service is not Active any more. Billing is stopped and Services can no longer be Delivered. Under certain conditions, a Terminated Contract/Subscribed Service can be Reinstated.

·         Never in Force: Used to cancel an Activation. The Contract/Subscribed Service is considered never having been Activated. All related Bills/Bill lines are cancelled.

·         On Hold: Temporary status that can be automatically set by hand or by the maintenance batch when data gets inconsistent. Billing and Service Delivery can be restricted.

·         On Hold After Renewal: Setup after the renewal process is completed, temporary status to enforce an automatic or manual review prior to Activation.

Customer Summary

Contracts are managed from the Person Summary screen. This screen is opened from the entry point Persons. The first tab is displaying information about the Contracts of a Person.

The push button subscribe Contract starts the process to create a new Contract.

On the right hand side of the list of Contracts Subscribed by the Person, common processes can be started:

·         Consult: to consult the selected Contract.

·         Modify: to modify the selected Contract.

·         History: to consult the history of the selected Contract.

·         Synthesis: to show summary of the selected Contract.

·         Print: to manually print the selected Contract.

·         Terminate: to manually Terminate the selected Contract.

·         Refresh: to manually Refresh the contract list.

·         Bill: to manually generate a Bill for the selected Contract.

·         Fees: to manually charge fees on the selected Contract.

·         Deliver: to deliver Services. A Subscribed Service of a Contract can allow for Delivered Services. For instance, Subscribed Coverages can allow the creation of claims.

Individual Contract screens

Main screen

The initial Effective date of the Contract defines when the Contract starts being Active. This date might be changed as long as the Contract status is Project (quote). When the Contract is Activated, the date can no longer be changed. This date must been included in the Marketing date  End Marketing date period of the Product.

The Expiration date defines when the Contract Termination is Effective.

The Can be Subscribed list displays the list of Services that are available. They are filtered by the subscription date field, as Offered Services within the Product also have their own Effective date  End Effective date period.

The Subscribed list displays Services that have actually been Subscribed, their current status and their Effective date/Termination date. Most common actions on a Subscribed Service are on the right:

·         Consult: consult the selected Subscribed Service

·         Modify: modify the selected Subscribed Service

·         Remove: remove the selected Subscribed Service (only possible if the Service or the Contract are still in project status)

·         Copy: create a new Subscribed Service from an existing one, the newly created one is created in project status, and is displayed to the user for validation.

·         Terminate: Terminate the selected Subscribed Service (only possible if both the Contract and the Service are Active)

Billing screen

This screen displays all Billing Services created for the Contract. Each Billing Service generates independent Bills. Usually only one Billing Service is created for a Contract, which will allow a bill to be generated for all Services at a chosen frequency. It is also possible to produce several Bills for the same Contract, if for example, different Payers exist. Refer to the chapter about Billing for more information.

Option subscription

From the list of available Options displayed, we can distinguish:

·         Mandatory Options: they are automatically Subscribed as soon as possible, that is at the Contract initial Effective date or the Offered Option Effective date. They cannot be removed or Terminated. If several instances of the same options are allowed, only the last instance cannot be removed, as there must be at least one Active instance as long as the Contract itself is Active.

·         Proposed Options: they are automatically Subscribed like mandatory Options, unless the user manually removes them.

·         Optional Options: they are not automatically Subscribed unless the user manually adds them.

·         To validate an Option, Wynsure checks :

o        Effective dates: the Option can only be Subscribed after its Effective date and before its end Effective date.

o        Subscription rules: Prerequired/Excludes constraints and eligibility rules.

·         When an Option is Subscribed, Wynsure checks if it can be automatically Activated. If user input is required, the Subscribed Option is left at a project status, so it can be modified to provide missing information and then Activated.

Main screen

This screen is very specific to the nature of the Option. Here we have the example of a Life Coverage with a Coverage amount history.

For each Coverage amount, the actions available include:

·         Consult: consult the selected Coverage amount

·         Modify: modify the selected Coverage amount

·         Remove: remove the selected Coverage amount (only possible if more than one coverage amount exists)

·         Copy: create a new Coverage amount  from an existing one

 

 A Life Coverage is defined through different amounts:

·         the requested Coverage amount displays depending on the Coverage amount rule of the Offered Coverage :

o        a fixed calculated amount

o        a combo box with a list of determined choices

o        an entry field were the user can input an amount.

·         the eligible Coverage amount corresponds to the requested amount with minimum/maximum rules being applied.

·         the automatic approved amount is an amount calculated by the underwriting rule. In this example $50,000

·         the remaining amount to underwrite = eligible amount  automatic approved amount.  In this example $10,000

·         the underwritten amount is an amount  that can be manually underwritten. A specific screen is displayed when you click on Manual Underwriting

·         the declined amount = remaining amount to underwrite  underwritten amount

·         the approved amount = automatic approved amount + underwritten amount (and also equals to eligible amount  declined amount)

The approved Coverage amount must be not null to be able to Activate the Subscribed Coverage.

Administration of the Subscribed Options

When the Contract is modified, the Subscribed Service can be managed with the following actions

Activation

A newly Subscribed Service is created in a project mode, so it needs to be Activated to be in force.

Termination

An Active Service can be Terminated under certain conditions

·         It is an Optional Service

·         It is mandatory but another instance of the Subscribed Service is already Active

·         It is required by another Subscribed Service but another instance of the Subscribed Service is already Active

·         Lapsing

·         Termination of the individual Contract

When the Termination screen displays, the user can check Never in Force.

If  Never in Force is checked, no other information is requested and the status if changed to Never in Force after validation.

If  Never in Force is not checked, the user must define the Termination date and a Termination reason. If the date is set in the past, the status will change to Terminate after validation. If the date is set in the future, the Termination is planned and will be fully Effective when the batch process runs at the scheduled Termination date.

Reinstatement

A Terminated Service can be Reinstated provided the Reinstatement delay setup on the Offered side has not expired.

Hold

An Active Service can be put on hold; it only acts as a marker.

Renewal

The renewal process of a Subscribed Service consists of different steps. The following diagram explains the different steps of the renewal process.

 



Active, No Renewal: the Service is Active and the renewal process has not started yet.

Active, Renewal process started: the Service is Active and a renewal process is in progress.

Active, Ready to send renewal notice: the Service is Active and the renewal process is completed (this step might be transient)

Active, Renewal notice sent: the Service is Active and the Contract maintenance batch has sent the renewal notice.

On hold after renewal, No renewal: the Service has reached its theoretical renewal date, so the Contract maintenance batch has renewed the Service. The Service is put on hold, and there is no renewal process anymore (this step might be transient)

Active, No renewal: the Service has been reviewed and reActivated again.

Activation

A new Contract is created in a project status, so it needs to be Activated for the Contract to be in force. Among controls done during Activation, at least one Subscribed Service must be Active and all Active Subscribed Services must be Billed.

Termination

An Active Contract can be Terminated any time. It cascades the Termination to all Active Subscribed Services. If the date is set in the past, the Contract is updated to reflect the change of status, but if the date is set in the future, the Contract is not updated, only a planned Termination is set up to be used by the Contract Maintenance Batch when the Termination date is reached.

Reinstatement

A Terminated Contract can be Reinstated provided the Reinstatement delay setup on the Product has not expired.

Hold

An Active Contract can be put on hold any time; it disables the Billing for that Contract.

Bill

Manually start the Billing process.

The Legal Entity insurance Contract is an agreement between a Legal Entity (the Subscriber) and an Issuing Company to get financial contribution against events (Fire, etc .). Wynsure handles this kind of Legal Entity Contracts in a way similar to Individual Contracts.

Group Benefit Plans have some specificities, and we will focus on them. A Group Benefit Plan is a special Legal Entity Insurance Contract, whose purpose is to provide insurance Benefits to its employees (or Members) of the Subscribing Legal Entity, who can be enrolled in a Group Benefit Plan.

In Wynsure, the Contract is created after selecting a Product and the Subscriber may choose among Coverages Offered by this Product.

Group Benefit Plan Contract and Subscribed Service Life Cycle

 

A Group Benefit Plan and a Subscribed Service (Coverages as well as other administrative Services) have a specific life cycle identified by a status and processes changing this status:

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


The different statuses are:

·         Project: Temporary state that does not bind the Subscriber with the issuing company. Can be used as a quote. Like a draft, information does not need to be consistent. It is not taken into account by the Billing and no Member can be enrolled.

·         Active: The agreement between the Subscriber and the issuing company has been reached. Services can be Billed and Members can be enrolled.

·         Terminated: The end of the Contract/Subscribed Service. Billing is stopped and Member enrolment is Terminated as well. Under certain conditions, a Terminated Contract/Subscribed Service can be Reinstated.

·         Never in Force: Used to cancel an Activation. The Contract/Subscribed Service is considered never having been Activated. All related Bills/Bill lines are cancelled. All Member enrolments are Terminated Never in Force

·         On Hold: Temporary status that can be automatically set by the maintenance batch when data gets inconsistent or manually set. Billing and Service delivery can be restricted. No impact on Member enrolments.

·         On Hold After Renewal: Setup after the renewal process is completed, temporary status to enforce an automatic or manual review prior to Activation. No impact on Member enrolments.

From the list of available Options displayed, we can distinguish:

·         Proposed Options: they are automatically Subscribed like mandatory Options, unless the user manually removes them.

·         Optional Options: they are not automatically Subscribed unless the user manually adds them.

·         To validate an Option, Wynsure checks :

o        Effective dates: the Option can only be Subscribed after its begin Effective date and before its end Effective date.

·         When an Option is Subscribed, Wynsure checks if it can be automatically Activated. If user input is required, the Subscribed Option is left at a project status, so it can be modified to provide missing information and then Activated.

A Terminated Service can be Reinstated provided the Reinstatement delay setup on the Offered side has not expired.

Hold

An Active Service can be put on hold; it only acts as a marker.

Renewal

The renewal process of a Subscribed Service consists of different steps. The following diagram explains the different steps of the renewal process.

 



Active, No Renewal: the Service is Active and the renewal process has not started yet.

Active, Renewal process started: the Service is Active and a renewal process is in progress.

Active, Ready to send renewal notice: the Service is Active and the renewal process is completed (this step might be transient)

Active, Renewal notice sent: the Service is Active and the Contract maintenance batch has sent the renewal notice.

On hold after renewal, No renewal: the Service has reached its theoretical renewal date, so the Contract maintenance batch has renewed the Service. The Service is put on hold, and there is no renewal process anymore (this step might be transient)

Active, No renewal: the Service has been reviewed and reActivated again.

Activation

A new Contract is created in a project status, so it needs to be Activated for the Contract to be in force. Among controls done during Activation, at least one Subscribed Service must be Active and all Active Subscribed Services must be Billed.

Termination

An Active Contract can be Terminated any time. It cascades the Termination to all Active Subscribed Services. If the date is set in the past, the Contract is updated to reflect the change of status, but if the date is set in the future, the Contract is not updated, only a planned Termination is set up to be used by the Contract Maintenance Batch when the Termination date is reached.

Reinstatement

A Terminated Contract can be Reinstated provided the Reinstatement delay setup on the Product has not expired.

Hold

An Active Contract can be put on hold any time; it disables the Billing for that Contract.

Affiliate relationship

 

As stated previously in this document, Wynsure defines an affiliation as a relationship between two entities whereby members of an organization affiliated with another organization may subscribe to the other organizations group benefit plans.   To put this in other words, if Company A is an affiliate of Company B, members of company A can subscribe to the customized service of the Group Benefit Plan of Company B.  What follows is a description of the procedure used to establish the affiliate relationship in Wynsure.

           

            Establish affiliation

 

From the Legal Entity modification screen of the legal entity chosen to be the affiliate, select the

Involvements tab.

 

 A new tab should appear on the right said labelled Affiliate.  Chose this tab and then create New. 

From here you will be prompted to enter the Legal Entity for which the current legal entity is to be the affiliate.  Begin and end dates for the affiliate relationship may also be specified.  Accept the changes made to the Legal Entity.

 

 

The next step is to specify the Group Benefit Plan for which it is desired to allow the Affiliated companys member to subscribe.   To do this select the Legal Entity summary screen for the company who now has affiliates. Select the Group Benefit Plan and choose the modify option.  From the Group Benefit Plan modification screen select the member management tab.  The bottom half of this tab contains a list of those affiliates.  Those affiliates with a checked box will be allowed to have their members subscribe to the GBP.

Once this option has been checked, members of the Affiliate will then be able to be enrolled to the Group Benefit Plan of the policy holder, providing they meet the subscription criteria that applies to all members of the Legal Entity.
