Accounting

 

Wynsure provides a rich accounting module that allows the management of client accounts. The billing module creates some bills that are attached to one client account. Then some payments are created and attached to a client account. Wynsure provides several reconciliation mechanisms to attach payments to bills. Every useful process are managed as transfer, write off, cancel, tracking

Definitions

A list of account lines, a balance, and two parties describe a Business account. These two parties are the issuing company and the customer. A positive line represents an amount due to the client. A negative line represents an amount due to the issuing company by the client.
A Billing account is an account specifically created attached to a Billing service to be the container of generated bills.
A Compensation Account is an account specifically created attached to a Compensation Service to be the container of generated Statements.
A period account is an account used to manage lines that are attached to a specific period defined by begin/end date.
A bill line is a line resulting on a contract billing.
A payment line is a line resulting on a payment is attached to one account.

Usage

Client accounts are created attached to:
Every billing service. The billing batch generates bills and bill lines are attached to the used billing service client account.
As soon as a Legal Entity is created, one suspense account is created automatically between the Legal entity and the Issuing Company. If several Issuing Companies are defined, several suspense accounts are created.
A Global Suspense Account is created for every Issuing Companies.

Definition

The billable element

The Billable Element is the base object for Billing. Several of them can be combined to define a Bill.
The Billable Element can be:

A subscribed Service

A delivered Service

 

The price can be:

Subscription Price (paid one time at Subscription time)

Periodic Price (paid every time a bill is created by the billing module)

Delivered Service Price (paid each time a Service is Delivered)

 

Common administrative fees are modelized with a specific offered service that can be delivered without being subscribed.

The bill

The billing module generates a bill. A bill is attached to the Client Account of the billing service defined in the contract that is billed. A bill is defined by a list of bill lines. These bill lines are directly appended into the client account as a bill line inherits from an account line. A bill line is generated at least for each Billable Element.

Usage

When a contract is defined, and the client must pay something to the issuing company, a bill is generated. This bill fills the contract client account.
In case of Compensation contract, at a given period, a compensation statement is created to define what is the compensation amount that has to be paid to the Broker. The compensation statement is a bill adapted to compensation.

Definition

Payment

A payment is used when some money arrives on an account. The payment contains information on its origin.

Payment form

A payment form is a complement for a payment. A payment can be of several types: credit card, check The payment form is used to save this specific information.

Bank deposit ticket

A bank deposit ticket contains information on a set of payment received from the bank: the batch number, total amount of the payments, lockbox, bank receive date and receive date, and lockbox.

Lock box payment

Wynsure provides a batch to load lockbox files (cf. lockbox batch section). For each batch received in the files, Wynsure creates a bank deposit ticket containing all the payments of the batch. This bank deposit ticket can be consulted from the bank deposit ticket entry point.

 

Usage

When a customer sends some money to the issuing company, the money can be received:

·         As a payment directly addressed to the issuing company. A simple payment will need to be created.

·         As a lock-box file, the bank receives the payment and processes it. It sends a lock box file to the issuing company. This file is treated by the lock box batch and appears in the application as bank deposit tickets containing payments.

·         As a payment part of a set: the payments are processed in the application as a bank deposit ticket created manually.

Reconciliation

When a client sends a payment to the insurance company, the payment is first attached to one target account then this money can be used to pay something due by the client. Due lines are lines with positive amount. Reconciliation is the process that attaches one payment or part of a payment to a due line. This due line is then partially or fully reconciled.

 

The user can choose to select manually the source of the money for the reconciliation or the source can be selected automatically by the application

Reconciliation can be:

Manual

Wynsure user must select the due line he wants to reconcile, and then he specifies the amount he wants to affect to this line. This amount is limited in one hand by the available suspended amount in the account and in the other hand by the still due amount of this line.

Automatic

Whatever the automatic reconciliation algorithm, reconciliation is firstly done by date. Oldest lines are treated first. When several lines are available for a defined date and the amount is not sufficient to pay the total amount of the lines, one of the following methods is used.

Split suspended amount

The available amount is split among the lines with some unpaid amount, proportionally to the unpaid amount of the line.

Smaller amount first

While some amount exists, this amount is allocated to the line with the smallest due amount. The amount is not split among lines with the same due amount.

Greater amount first

This method is the same as Smaller amount first but the amount is first allocated to the line with the greatest due amount.

Use priorities

The available amount is allocated using the priorities defined using the reconciliation priority manager.

Reconciliation priority manager

 

Wynsure user can define priorities for each offered service using the priority manager. To define these priorities, the user just needs to order the options. The options without priority are treated as the options with the lowest priority. The user can:

·         Append a selected option without priority to the list of options with priority (The option will have the lowest priority, i.e. the larger value);

·         Insert a selected option at with a specified priority;

·         Move up, down or remove a selected option with priority;

·         Purge the list of options with priorities;

·         Reset the manager (All changes will be rolled back and the priorities will be reset to their last saved state);

·         Apply the priorities: the changes will be saved.

If the user checks the Change priority of option when is equal option, all the existing options with the same or a lowest priority will be shift.

Bill Cancel

From the client summary screen, it is possible to see the list of bills attached to this client. From this list, the user can select the last generated bill and click on Cancel action. This action is removing all bill lines from the account and readjusts the account balance.
When a bill is cancelled, it is possible to recalculate it from the billing module.

Payment cancel

From the client summary screen, it is possible to see the list of payments created by this client. From this list, the user can select one payment and click on Cancel action. This action is removing the corresponding payment line from the account and readjusts the account balance. If a Money transfer was done to move the payment to another account, the transfer need to be cancelled to cancel the payment.
If this payment has been used to reconcile a bill, the reconciliation is undone, so has there is enough money in suspense to cancel the payment.

Write Off

When a bill is generated, and some due amount exists, it is possible to cancel this due amount. Of course the generated bill is still valid, but the accounting system behaves exactly as the client had paid this amount.

Bill line

Write off at bill line level apply to the unpaid amount of this bill line. The user specifies the amount of the write off, so the unpaid amount can be partially written off.

 

Example:
Bill line:
Amount: 100$
Paid amount 10$ (reconciliation with a payment)
Write off amount 20$ (specified by the user authorized to do a write off)
Still due amount: 70$

Bill

The write off at bill level automatically applies to all unpaid bill lines. Each bill line unpaid amount (can be already partially reconciled or written off) is written off. So when a bill is written off, the global bill status is <Paid>.

Money Transfer

From Client Account to Client Account

Wynsure user can move some money from one client account to another client account.

The user needs to select an origin for the transfer. When this origin is selected, it is not possible to move an amount greater that the amount available from the origin.

From/To Client Account From/to GL Account (General ledger account)

You can define a transfer from a client account to a General Ledger account. It means that the client account balance is updated and a line is generated to update the General Ledger external module.
This kind of transfer can be defined in the two directions:

·         From one client account to General Ledger

·         From General ledger to one Client account

 

The General Ledger is impacted depending on the reason that is specified:

·         Bank reversal

·         Overpayment

·         Reinstatement denial

·         Retroactive adjustment

·         Termination

Payment tracking

From Payments list (Actor summary or Payments queries) the user can select one payment and click on <tracking> action button.

The top part of the tracking screen presents the tree of all actions that have been done with this payment.
When the user selects one line in the tree, he can see the detail concerning this line: the concerned account, the operation date and where does this money come from.
Different bitmaps are used to display Cash deposits into the account, reconciliations, Transfers.
